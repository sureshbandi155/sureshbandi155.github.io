Introduction

Welcome to DGA's keypad tutorial.

This interactive tutorial will show you how to arm and disarm your alarm system.

You can also follow these instructions to learn more advanced functions including how to bypass a zone, reset sensors, monitor a zone, and test your system.

Some keypads control multiple alarm systems. In these cases, your display will prompt you to specify whether commands apply to specific systems, or to all of them. Click the button below for the next section.

Keypad tutorial

Your DGA keypad is easy to use. The green light at the upper left (POWER) tells you that your system has power.

The red light beneath it (ARMED) comes on only when a system is armed, and the light goes out when you disarm it.

Your display should show the time and will prompt you with instructions.

In this tutorial, follow the instructions using your mouse on the keypad shown here.

Click the button below for the next section.

How to arm your alarm system

Follow the instructions using your mouse on the keypad shown here.

  1. Press the CMD key until the display reads: ARM DISARM.
  2. Press the key beneath ARM.
  3. When the display reads ENTER CODE, enter your keypad code (Use 1-2-3-4 for this tutorial).
  4. Next, finish by pressing CMD.
  5. After the display counts down and the red ARMED lights goes on, your system is armed.

    6. Click the button below for the next section.

How to disarm your alarm system

Follow the instructions using your mouse on the keypad shown here.

Most often you will need to disarm your system after you enter your premises.

  1. Press the CMD key until the display reads: ARM DISARM.
  2. Press the key beneath DISARM.
  3. When the display reads ENTER CODE, enter your keypad code (Use 1-2-3-4 for this tutorial).
  4. Next, finish by pressing CMD.
  5. When the red ARMED light goes out, your system is disarmed.

replay

How to bypass a zone

Follow the instructions using your mouse on the keypad shown here.

Sometimes you may need to bypass a zone. For example, you need to arm your alarm system but can't get a window to close properly. To bypass a zone:

  1. Press CMD until ARM DISARM appears on the keypad display, then press CMD again for MENU.
  2. Press the button beneath YES to access the menu, then enter your keypad code (Use 1-2-3-4 for this tutorial).
  3. Press CMD to see the menu.
  4. Keep pressing CMD until you reach BYPASS ZONES (If you skip past it, use your button to go back to it).
  5. 5.Press any button beneath the display to select BYPASS ZONES.
  6. 6.Enter the zone you need to bypass (Use 7 for this tutorial).
  7. 7.Press the button beneath BYP to bypass zone 7.
  8. 8.Finish by pressing the key to exit the menu. (You can bypass up to three zones, but never priority zones like safes.)

    9. Before bypassing a zone, contact the monitoring center to confirm that part of your alarm system will be unarmed.

    Click the button below for the next section.

How to reset your alarm system's sensors

Follow the instructions using your mouse on the keypad shown here.

To cancel zone bypasses and return your alarm system to its normal, disarmed status, reset all the sensors. To reset the sensors:

  1. Press CMD until ARM DISARM appears on the keypad display, then press CMD again for the MENU.
  2. Press the button beneath YES to access the menu, then enter your keypad code (Use 1-2-3-4 for this tutorial).
  3. Press CMD to see the menu.
  4. Press CMD again to reach SENSOR RESET.
  5. Press any button beneath the display to reset the sensors.
  6. 6.The sensors are reset and your alarm system is now back to its normal, disarmed status.

    7. Click the button below for the next section.

How to monitor a zone

Follow the instructions using your mouse on the keypad shown here.

When your alarm system is disarmed, you may want your keypad to chime when a particular zone's sensor is activated (alerting you whenever the front door opens, for example).

To do this, set your keypad to monitor any or all of your zones. To monitor a zone:

  1. Press CMD until ARM DISARM appears on the keypad display, then press CMD again for MENU.
  2. Press the button beneath YES to access the menu, then enter your keypad code (Use 1-2-3-4 for this tutorial).
  3. 3.Press CMD to see the menu.
  4. 4.Keep pressing CMD to reach ZONE MONITOR (If you skip past it, use your button to go back to it).
  5. 5.Press any button beneath the display to select ZONE MONITOR.
  6. 6.Press the button beneath NBR to monitor specific zones, rather than all zones.
  7. 7.Enter the zone you want to monitor (Use 7 for this tutorial). Then press the button beneath ADD to monitor a zone.

    8. (You can follow these steps to remove any monitored zones by selecting RMV instead of ADD).

  8. 8.Finish by pressing the key until your keypad display returns to normal.
  9. 9.Zone 7 is now monitored, so your keypad will chime whenever its sensor is activated.

    10. Click the button below for the next section.

How to test your alarm system

Follow the instructions using your mouse on the keypad shown here.

You can test your alarm system, including your connection to the monitoring center, directly from your keypad. To run a test:

  1. Press CMD until ARM DISARM appears on the keypad display, then press CMD again for MENU.
  2. Press the button beneath YES to access the menu, then enter your passcode (Use 1-2-3-4 for this tutorial).
  3. Press CMD to see the menu.
  4. Keep pressing CMD to reach SYSTEM TEST (If you skip past it, use your button to go back to it).
  5. 5.Press any button beneath the display to select SYSTEM TEST.
  6. 6.Press the button beneath SYSTEM to test your system.(Selecting PANICS allows you to manually press panic buttons without activating your alarm).
  7. 7.Your test is complete and your alarm system has resumed normal function.
