Introduction

Welcome to DGA's keypad tutorial.

This interactive tutorial will show you how to arm and disarm your alarm system.

You can also follow these instructions to learn more advanced functions including how to bypass a point, reset sensors, setup watch mode, and test your system.

Click the button below for the next section.

Keypad tutorial

Your DGA keypad is easy to use. The display will tell you wheather your system is ready to arm and will prompt you with instructions.

Use the ENT key to enter codes and to answer YES to questions from the display.

Use the COMMAND key to begin advanced functions.

Use the ESC key to cancel any command at any time.

Click the button below for the next section.

How to arm
your alarm system

Follow the instructions using your mouse on the keypad shown here.

  1. Enter your keypad code (Use 1-2-3-4 for this tutorial).
  2. Next, finish by pressing ENT.

    3. Your display will begin counting down until it is armed. To avoid activating your alarm, make sure you leave your premises before the countdown ends.
    Your alarm system is now armed.

    Click the button below for the next section.

How to disarm
your alarm system

Follow the instructions using your mouse on the keypad shown here.

Entering your premises will activate a sensor. You'll need to disarm your system within a set period of time to avoid activating your alarm.

(Your keypad will display which sensor has been activated).

  1. To disarm your system, enter your keypad code (Use 1-2-3-4 for this tutorial).
  2. Next, finish by pressing ENT.

Your system is disarmed.

Although there is no need to stop your keypad from showing the alarm history (ALARM SILNCED / A1 1 ALARMS), you can clear it from your display by entering your keypad code again and pressing ESC.

Click the button below for the next section.

How to bypass a point

Follow the instructions using your mouse on the keypad shown here.

Telling your system to ignore signals from one of yours sensors is called bypassing a point. You can bypass up to three points, but never priority points like safes.

  1. To bypass a point, press COMMAND and then 0.
  2. Enter your keypad code (Use 1-2-3-4 for this tutorial) then press ENT to continue.
  3. Enter the number of the point you want to bypass (Use 7 for this tutorial) than press ENT to continue.
  4. 4.Finish by pressing ENT again to confirm that this is the point you want to bypass.

    5. Point 7 will now be bypassed untill you cancel the bypass.

    (Before bypassing a point, contact the monitoring center to confirm that part of your alarm system will be unarmed.)

  5. 5. To cancle this bypass, press COMMAND and then 00.
  6. 6.Enter your keypad code (Use 1-2-3-4 for this tutorial) then press ENT to continue. .
  7. 7.To cancle the bypass, enter the number of the bypassed point (Use 7 for this tutorial) then press ENT to continue.

    8. Your display will identify the point whose bypass you are canceling.

  8. 8.Finish by pressing ENT again to confirm that this is the bypassed point you want to cancel.

    9. Now your system will stop ingoring signals from the sensor at point 7.

    Click the button below for the next section.

How to reset
your sensors

Follow the instructions using your mouse on the keypad shown here.

  1. To reset your alarm system's sensors, press COMMAND and then 47.
  2. Your system is now resetting all sensors .

    3. Your sensors are reset and your system is operating normally.

    Click the button below for the next section.

How to set watch mode

Follow the instructions using your mouse on the keypad shown here.

When your alarm system is disarmed, you may want your keypad to chime when a particular zone's sensor is activated (alerting you whenever your doors open, for example).

  1. To do this, set your alarm system to watch mode by pressing COMMAND and then 6.

    2. Watch mode is now on, and your keypad will chime when your doors open.

  2. To turn off watch mode, repeat these steps. Press COMMAND and then 6.

    3. Watch mode is now off.

How to test
your alarm system

Follow the instructions using your mouse on the keypad shown here.

There are two commands that test your alarm system. One tests your sensors, and the other tests your system's connection to the monitoring center.

  1. To test your alarm system's sensors, press COMMAND and then 44.

    2. Your display will show you how many points (sensors) it will test, and then identify each as it is activated.

    when all sensors are tested, your display will return to its normal state.

  2. 3. To test your alarm system's connection to the monitoring center, press COMMAND and then 41.

    3. Your system will send a report to the monitoring center to confirm connectivity.

    Your display will confirm that the report was sent to the monitoring center.

    DGA recommends contacting the monitoring center to confirm that the report was received. Click the button below for the next section.

replay

How to change
your keypad code

Follow the instructions using your mouse on the keypad shown here.

Any keypad code can be changed directly through your keypad.

  1. To change a keypad code, press COMMAND and then 55.
  2. Next, enter your keypad code, then press ENT (Use 1-2-3-4 for this tutorial).
  3. Enter your new keypad code, then press ENT (Use 5-6-7-8 for this tutorial).
  4. Enter your new keypad code again to confirm it, then press ENT (Use 5-6-7-8 for this tutorial).

    5. Your keypad code has been changed to 5-6-7-8.

    Click the button below for the next section.

replay

How to force arm
your alarm system

Follow the instructions using your mouse on the keypad shown here.

On rare occasions you may need to arm your alarm system when a sensor in a non-priority zone is malfunctioning, preventing you from arming normally.

  1. Start by entering your keypad code as you normally would to arm your system (Use 1-2-3-4 for this tutorial).
  2. Next, press ENT to begin the arming process.

    3. If your system is not ready to arm, your keypad display will prompt you to check the area. (Pressing NEXT will display the zone that is preventing the system from arming normally.)

    Your display will then ask whether you want to force arm the system.

  3. 3. Press COMMAND then ENT to proceed.

    4. Your keypad display will begin counting down untill it is armed.

    Your alarm system is now armed, but with at least one disabled sensor. You should contact the monitoring center to confirm that part of your alarm system will be unarmed.
